Most CRMs are databases. You put data in, you get data out. If nobody types anything, nothing happens.

Octolane is the opposite. It runs like a sales team. Its agents are constantly working - scanning your email, monitoring your website, searching the web, and acting on what they find. They escalate to you only when they need a human decision.

## The self-driving loop

Octolane runs a continuous cycle across your entire revenue operation:

### 1. Find

Octolane finds prospects and opportunities from every direction:

- **Email and calendar** - the Deal Finder Agent scans your inbox and meetings for signals that a deal is forming
- **Your website** - the Signal Agent identifies which companies are visiting your site right now, even if they never fill out a form
- **Form submissions** - the Form Submission Agent captures every inbound lead the moment they submit
- **The web** - Octolane constantly searches the web for relevant signals about your prospects, market, and competitors
- **250+ data sources** - web analytics, marketing tools, support platforms, and more feed data into your CRM automatically

### 2. Qualify

Every prospect that enters your pipeline gets evaluated automatically:

- Matched against your ideal customer profile
- Enriched with company size, funding, job titles, tech stack, and social profiles
- Scored based on engagement signals - website visits, email opens, meeting activity
- Routed to the right pipeline stage based on their readiness

### 3. Act

Octolane doesn't just tell you what to do - it does it:

- The **Follow-Up Agent** drafts emails when conversations go quiet - in your voice, with the right context
- The **Outreach Agent** sends personalized messages to qualified prospects
- The **Meeting Recorder** joins your calls and captures every detail

### 4. Escalate

Agents don't go rogue. When they draft an email, suggest an action, or need a judgment call, it shows up in your **approval queue**. You review, approve, edit, or override with one tap. Nothing happens without your permission.

## Where Octolane gets its data

Octolane isn't limited to what your team types in. It pulls data from:

- **Gmail and Google Calendar** - emails, meetings, calendar events
- **Your website** - visitor traffic via the Signal script
- **Web analytics tools** - page views, referral sources, engagement data
- **LinkedIn** - connections, prospect activity, profile data
- **WhatsApp** - messaging conversations
- **The web** - Octolane's agents actively search the internet for prospect intelligence, company updates, and market signals
- **250+ additional sources** via direct integrations and Zapier

This means your CRM is always current. Contacts are enriched in real-time. Deals are updated as conversations happen. Pipeline stages reflect reality, not what someone remembered to log last Friday.

## How agents work together

Agents don't operate in isolation. They hand off work to each other:

1. **Signal Agent** identifies a company visiting your pricing page three times this week
2. It passes the company to the **Deal Finder Agent**, which checks if there's already a conversation in your email
3. If no existing thread is found, the **Outreach Agent** drafts a personalized message based on the company's profile and browsing behavior
4. The message shows up in your approval queue - you tap approve
5. After the prospect replies, the **Follow-Up Agent** monitors the thread and drafts responses if things go quiet
6. When a meeting is booked, the **Meeting Recorder** joins the call and captures action items

This is a full sales cycle - from anonymous website visitor to active deal - with minimal human effort.

## You stay in control

Octolane is self-driving, not autonomous. You decide:

- Which agents are active
- What criteria qualify a lead
- Whether emails go out (every draft needs your approval)
- How your pipeline stages are structured
- What data sources are connected

Think of it like managing a team. You set the strategy, define the rules, and review the work. The agents handle the execution.
