# Octolane vs Pipedrive

Pipedrive is a popular CRM for small sales teams. It's simple, visual, and focused on pipeline management. For teams who want a clean drag-and-drop pipeline view, Pipedrive delivers.

But Pipedrive is still a manual CRM. You create deals, you update stages, you log calls, you write follow-ups. The pipeline only reflects reality if your team keeps it updated. Octolane removes the manual work entirely.

## The core difference

Pipedrive is a **visual pipeline tool**. It makes it easy to see and manage your deals - but only if someone puts the deals there and keeps them updated.

Octolane is a **self-driving CRM**. It finds deals from your email and calendar, identifies companies visiting your website, drafts follow-ups when conversations go quiet, and keeps every record enriched and current - without your team touching it.

## Where Pipedrive falls short

### Everything is manual

In Pipedrive, you manually create deals, manually update stages, manually add contacts, and manually log activities. The "Activities" feature reminds you to do things - but you still have to do them.

Octolane's agents do these things for you. Deals are detected automatically. Contacts are created from email and calendar interactions. Activities are logged in real-time. Pipeline stages update based on actual conversation signals.

### No deal detection

Pipedrive doesn't know about a deal until you create one. If a prospect emails you about pricing and you forget to add it to Pipedrive, that deal doesn't exist in your pipeline.

Octolane's Deal Finder agent reads your email, calendar, and 250+ connected data sources. When it detects buying signals - pricing inquiries, demo requests, contract discussions - it creates the deal automatically. You never miss an opportunity because you forgot to log it.

### No website visitor tracking

Pipedrive has a web form integration and a basic website tracking add-on (LeadBooster), but it can't identify which companies are visiting your site in real-time the way Octolane's Signal does.

Signal identifies anonymous companies browsing your website - even if they never fill out a form - matches them against your ideal customer profile, and can trigger automated outreach to qualified visitors.

### Follow-ups require discipline

Pipedrive's activity reminders help, and its email automation features let you send templated sequences. But if a deal goes quiet and nobody notices, Pipedrive doesn't do anything about it.

Octolane's Follow-Up Agent watches every conversation. When a thread goes dark, it drafts a personalized follow-up in your voice - not a template - and queues it for your approval.

### Contact enrichment is limited

Pipedrive's contact data is whatever you type in or import. Enrichment requires third-party add-ons.

Octolane enriches every contact and company from the live web automatically - job titles, company size, funding data, social profiles, tech stack - and keeps it current.

## What Pipedrive does better

Pipedrive has a simpler, more visual pipeline interface that some teams prefer for drag-and-drop deal management. It also has a larger marketplace of add-ons and integrations, a built-in calling feature, and more mature email marketing tools.

Pipedrive's mobile app is well-regarded for field sales teams who need to log activities on the go.

## Who should switch to Octolane

Octolane is the better choice if:

- You're tired of manually updating deal stages and contact records
- You want your CRM to find deals and follow up automatically
- You want to know which companies are browsing your website before they reach out
- You're a small team that wants sales execution without the busywork
- You want a modern CRM that does the work, not just organizes it

## Migration

Export your Pipedrive data from **Settings → Export data**, then import the CSV into Octolane. Your deals, contacts, and companies come over cleanly. From there, Octolane's agents take over.
