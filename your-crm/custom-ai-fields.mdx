Custom AI Fields are fields that populate themselves. You define what information you want to track, and Octolane extracts it from your emails, meetings, website data, and connected tools.

## How it works

1. **Create a field** - give it a name and tell Octolane what to look for (in plain English)
2. **Octolane scans your data** - it reads emails, meeting transcripts, website activity, and enrichment data to find the answer
3. **The field fills in** - the value appears on the relevant record and stays updated as new data comes in

## Examples

- **Budget range** - "What budget or price range has the prospect mentioned?" → Octolane scans email threads and meeting transcripts for pricing discussions
- **ICP tier** - "Is this company a Tier 1, 2, or 3 fit based on our criteria?" → Octolane evaluates company size, industry, and engagement signals
- **Buying timeline** - "When does this prospect want to make a decision?" → Octolane looks for timeline mentions in conversations
- **Decision maker** - "Who is the final decision maker?" → Octolane identifies key stakeholders from email and meeting participants
- **Competitor mentioned** - "Has the prospect mentioned any competitors?" → Octolane flags competitive mentions in threads

## Creating a Custom AI Field

Go to **Settings → Data Model → Add Field → AI Field**. Describe what you want the field to capture in plain English. Choose which object it belongs to (deal, contact, or company) and Octolane starts filling it in across your records.
